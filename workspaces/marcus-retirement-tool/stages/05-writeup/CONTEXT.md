# Stage 05: Competition Writeup

Write the 100-word competition writeup. Last step before submission.

## Inputs

| Source | File | Scope | Why |
|--------|------|-------|-----|
| Marcus brief | `../../inputs/marcus-brief.md` | "Your Deliverable" + "What We're Looking For" | Competition criteria |
| Story map | `../02-story-framework/output/story-map.md` | Stage 0 + end-state messages | Confirm what the tool actually does |

## Process

1. Draft a writeup covering exactly three things (competition requirement):
   - **Who it's for:** Marcus's prospects — 35–55, mostly dual-income families, about to have their first planning meeting with Marcus
   - **What it does:** A 5-stage camping trip adventure that collects five financial inputs and reveals whether they'll make camp before dark; results show the exact route to fix it
   - **One design choice + why:** Choose the strongest of these options:
     - The camping trip metaphor — non-threatening for already-worried prospects; "the end is always salvageable" maps naturally to a route adjustment, not a verdict
     - The three-phase day-to-night visual arc — the visual journey mirrors narrative urgency; prospects feel the stakes without being told to feel them
     - Mechanical choice buttons — speed of a multiple choice quiz with the warmth of a trail decision; no blank forms

2. Target: as close to 100 words as possible (98–102 acceptable)

3. Read it aloud in Marcus's voice — if it sounds like a bank brochure, rewrite

4. Verify against competition criteria:
   - Does it solve Marcus's actual problem? (prospect uses it before the call, shows up prepared)
   - Does it sound like Marcus? (honest, direct, human — not financial-services tone)
   - Simple enough to finish? (five inputs, under three minutes)

## Audit

| Check | Pass Condition |
|-------|----------------|
| Word count | 98–102 words |
| Three required elements | Who it's for, what it does, one design choice + why — all present |
| Voice | Passes the "sounds like Marcus" test |
| Competition criteria | All three judging criteria addressed implicitly or explicitly |

## Outputs

| Artifact | Location | Format |
|----------|----------|--------|
| writeup.md | `output/writeup.md` | Three labeled sections, ~33 words each, ~100 words total |

**Output format:** Three `## Heading` sections matching the page section labels exactly:
- `## Who it's for`
- `## What it does`
- `## Design Choices`

Each section is a single short paragraph (~33 words). No sub-headers, no bullets. Stage 04 slots each section into the corresponding brand page section by label match.

When approved, copy to `../../deliverables/writeup.md`.
